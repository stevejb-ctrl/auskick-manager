You're coaching U13s. 18 on the field — the jump to senior-shape
footy. Position model is "positions5": back, half-back, centre,
half-forward, forward. Kids are 12-13, voices breaking, attention
spans longer, and they're starting to identify with positions
("I'm a hard-running half-back").

What's distinctive at U13:

- Five zones, not three. The complexity of the lineup picker
  jumps. A coach now needs to differentiate "back" from
  "half-back" — different roles, different kids.
- Specialisation begins. Some kids are clearly mids, some
  defenders. The suggester can't just rotate everyone everywhere
  — it needs to respect specialisation while still rotating
  fairly.
- Real injury risk. 18 kids running into each other at age 13
  produces actual concussions. The injury flow has to be
  fast and visible (parents on the sideline need to see "yes
  the coach saw it, kid's coming off").
- Stats start mattering for the kids' own development. They
  look at their own minutes, goals, and where they played.
