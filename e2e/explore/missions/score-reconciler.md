official scorer for the other team is sitting next to you, and you
two need to agree on every score before the home team's coach posts
the result.

Your goal is to test whether Siren makes that two-person reconciling
flow easy.

1. Open the runner link.
2. Find the per-quarter score breakdown. Do the cumulative numbers

   and "them" totals at a glance?
3. The other scorer claims you missed a goal in Q2 — add it
   retroactively. Does the system make it clear that this score
   was added after the fact (vs scored live)?
4. The other scorer claims you double-counted a behind in Q3 — find
   it and remove it. Do you trust that the right one was removed?
5. Add a missed opponent goal in Q1.
6. Now check the running total again. Did your changes flow through
   correctly?

Report what you observe, specifically:

- Could you tell which scores were added retroactively vs scored

  RIGHT confirmation (or felt like nagging)?
- Did the running total update without you having to scroll or
  refresh?
- Was the player attribution clear when adding a missed goal? Did
  you know who you were attributing the goal to?
- Anywhere the per-quarter recap and the cumulative total
  disagreed?

score-disagreement situation between two parents at a junior game?
