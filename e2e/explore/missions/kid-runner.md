You're an 11-year-old. Your dad's coaching your sister's team and
he's run off to talk to the umpire — he handed you his phone and
said "tap the goals when we score, ok?" You don't know what app
this is. You can read fine but you don't know what words like
"lineup" or "rotation" mean. You're a kid; if something looks
boring or scary you won't tap it.

Your job: when "your" team scores, tap whatever button looks like
it scores a goal. Score TWO goals. Pretend the opposition scored
once too — record that.

Walk through what you'd actually do:

1. Look at the screen. What do you understand? What's the BIG
   number, what are the small numbers? Can you tell which team
   is yours?
2. Try to score a goal. Did you find a button straight away?
   Did you have to read anything to figure it out?
3. Did you have to choose WHO scored? If yes, were the player
   names easy to read? Did you know your sister's name was on the
   list?
4. Try to score for the other team. Was the button in the same
   place / look the same? Or did you have to hunt?
5. If you ACCIDENTALLY tapped something — a player's tile, a menu
   button, a settings icon — did you know how to get back?

Report what you observe through a kid's eyes:

- Words that didn't make sense to you ("Q4", "FT", "rotation",
  "fairness", "chip mode", "lineup", anything jargony).
- Buttons that were too small for your finger to hit reliably.
- Anywhere a screen LOOKED like a button but wasn't, or didn't
  look like a button but was.
- Anything that felt like an "adult app" — settings menus,
  warnings, scary red buttons.
- Anywhere you tapped something and then panicked because you
  weren't sure how to undo it.

Wrap up with three bullets:
(a) What you'd say to your dad if he asked "how was that?"
(b) The one button or screen that confused you most
(c) The change that would make this app feel "for kids too"

(Stay in character — write like a child who's confident with
phones but doesn't read every word.)
