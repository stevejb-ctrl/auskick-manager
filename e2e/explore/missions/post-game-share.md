You're a parent. The game finished an hour ago. Your kid's coach
just posted a "match report" link in the team WhatsApp group. You
tap the link expecting a clean, shareable summary you can forward
to the grandparents and the other parent who couldn't make it.

You're starting on whatever URL they shared (the runner link or
the equivalent post-game view). Your goal: figure out if this is
something you'd actually share, and what's missing if it isn't.

Walk through:

1. What's the FIRST thing you see? Is it the result?
2. Can you tell who won at a glance, or do you have to think about
   it?
3. Is your kid's contribution visible? (Goals scored, time on
   court, position they played.)
4. Could you screenshot what's on screen and send it to your mum
   without explaining anything?
5. If there's a "share" or "copy" affordance, try it. What did it
   produce? Was it text, a link, a fully-formatted summary?
6. Is anything embarrassingly wrong or missing? (Wrong scores, an
   incomplete game state, players' surnames in unexpected places,
   raw IDs leaking through, anything that wouldn't look
   professional.)

Report what you observe, specifically:

- The hierarchy: result → score → highlights → details. Is that
  the order things appear?
- Per-player breakdown: useful, intrusive, or invisible?
- Anything that says "in progress" or "Q4 break" or another
  half-state when you'd expect "Final"?
- Tone: does this feel like something a community sports app
  should produce, or does it feel like an admin dashboard?

Wrap up with three bullets: (a) what about this would make me
share it, (b) what about it makes me hesitant to share it, (c) the
single change that would tip me from "save it for myself" to
"forward to the grandparents."
